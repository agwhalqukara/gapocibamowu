Michael Burden Overview and Wiki

Michael Burden, an acclaimed Australian musicologist, has carved a distinguished career in the United Kingdom, particularly at the University of Oxford. His influence extends beyond academia, touching on significant areas in opera and music history.

Early Life and Education

Born in Adelaide, South Australia, Burden’s early education was at Pulteney Grammar School followed by the University of Adelaide. He furthered his studies with a PhD from the University of Edinburgh, setting the stage for a robust academic career.

Academic and Leadership Roles

Michael Burden’s academic journey has been rich and varied. He serves as a Fellow in Music, Dean, and Chattels Fellow at New College, Oxford. His roles extend beyond administrative duties, encompassing significant contributions to both teaching and research.

Research Focus

Michael Burden’s research interests are deeply rooted in the history of stage music, particularly exploring the works of Henry Purcell and the broader aspects of dance and theatre from the 17th to the 19th centuries.

Contributions to Music Editions

Michael Burden’s scholarly output includes significant contributions to the editions of works by notable composers.

He has been involved in editing and overseeing the production of collected music editions for composers like Alessandro Stradella and John Eccles, ensuring that their works are accessible and accurately represented for contemporary scholars and performers.

His own editorial projects include seminal works such as:
