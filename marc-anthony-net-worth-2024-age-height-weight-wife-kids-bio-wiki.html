Early Life

Marco Antonio Muñiz, better known as Marc Anthony, is the son of Guillermina and Felipe Muñiz. He was born in New York City on 16 September 1968. He spent his childhood in Harlem and is a Roman Catholic.

Career

Marc Anthony released ‘Rebel’ in 1988. In 1991, he came up with ‘When the Night is Over.’ This was followed by the albums ‘Otra Nota,’ ‘Todo a Su Tiempo,’ and ‘Contra la Corriente.’ His self-titled album was a commercial success and was certified platinum.

He then released ‘Libre’ in 2001. The following year, he came up with ‘Mended.’ In 2004, he released the albums ‘Amar Sin Mentiras’ and ‘Valio la Pena.’ His album ‘El Cantante’ became available in 2007. In 2010, the singer released ‘Iconos.’ His most recent studio album ‘’3.0’ was released in 2013.

Anthony has also released four compilation albums, including ‘Desde un Principio: From the Beginning’ and ‘Sigo Siendo Yo (Grandes Exitos).’ He also released two live albums in 2007.

He has released many singles including “Hasta Que Te Conoci,” “Ride on the Rhythm,” “Y Hubo Alguien,” “I Need To Know,” “You Sang to Me,” “Mi Gente,” “Vivir Mi Vida,” “Cambio de Peil,” and “Flor Palida.” He has appeared as a featured performer for several other artists like Tina Arena, Jennifer Lopez, Pitbull, Alejandro Sanz, Maluma, and Juan Gabriel.

In films, Anthony has appeared in movies like ‘Natural Causes,’ ‘The Substitute,’ ‘In the Time of Butterflies,’ ‘Man on Fire,’ and ‘El Cantante.’ He has appeared on the television shows ‘The X Factor’ and ‘Hawthorne.’

Awards & Achievements

Marc Anthony has won the Grammy Awards twice in his career. He has won the Latin Grammy Award seven times and is the winner of three American Music Awards. He has also won several Billboard Latin Music Awards and Lo Nuestro Awards. In 2011, he was inducted into the Ride of Fame. In 2009, he was conferred with the CHCI Chair’s Lifetime Achievement Award.
