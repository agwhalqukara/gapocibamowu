Saluche Full Overview and Wiki

The Humble Beginnings

Born in 1948 in Kingstown, Saint Vincent, Lucien Small, affectionately known as Saluche, embarked on a journey that would see him crowned the King of Comedy in Saint Vincent.

His early foray into the workforce as a warehouse manager for Cable & Wireless showcased his dedication and work ethic. However, it was his unparalleled talent for comedy that would eventually define his career.

Early Life and Career Beginnings

Navigating life’s challenges from an early age, he took on the responsibility of helping raise his younger twin brothers following their father’s death in 1969.

His early career at Cable & Wireless, culminating in a managerial position, provided him with the discipline and people skills that later augmented his comedic persona.

His transformation from Sir Lucien to Saluche marked the beginning of a brand that would become synonymous with Vincentian comedy.

Rise to Fame

His comedic journey gained momentum in Trinidad and Tobago in the early 1990s, where he first achieved recognition.

His return to Saint Vincent saw him leveraging everyday observations into humorous anecdotes, resonating with a wide audience.

Jokes about the seashore being the best place for laundry due to the free Tide, Surf, and Breeze exemplified his ability to turn mundane observations into laughter, establishing him as a household name in the Caribbean comedy scene.

His catchphrase, You got to clap for that! became synonymous with his performances, echoing through the halls of every venue he graced.

Contributions and Controversies

His role as the host of Night Riders on Nice Radio further cemented Saluche’s place in Vincentian culture, offering a platform for both established and upcoming artists.

However, his comedic takes on political matters led to a clash with the ruling Unity Labour Party, resulting in a blacklist that significantly impacted his career.

Despite these challenges, His resilience shone through as he sought support from the opposition New Democratic Party, showcasing his unwavering commitment to his craft.

Personal Life

Helen, an office manager for The Vincentian newspaper, shared a life with Saluche that was filled with mutual respect and understanding.

FAQs about Saluche

Who was Saluche?

Saluche, born Lucien Small in 1948, was a celebrated Vincentian comedian, storyteller, and radio personality renowned for his observational comedy and satire that often reflected on the culture of Saint Vincent and the Grenadines, current events, and everyday life.

What made he famous?

He earned the title King of Comedy in Saint Vincent due to his regular appearances at local and regional festivals, captivating audiences with his unique humor and engaging storytelling.

How did he begin his career?

He first gained recognition as a comedian in Trinidad and Tobago in the early 1990s before returning to Saint Vincent, where he continued to build his reputation through performances at various festivals and events.

Did he face any challenges in his career?

Yes, in 2006, he faced significant challenges when the ruling Unity Labour Party blacklisted him from government jobs for telling political jokes at their expense, which drastically reduced his public performances.

How did he contribute to the community?

Beyond his comedy, he hosted Night Riders, a radio program that aired every Thursday on Nice Radio, featuring his own material as well as pieces sent in by listeners and upcoming artists.

When did Saluche pass away?

He died of a heart attack at his home in Dorsetshire Hill on January 14, 2007, at the age of 58.
